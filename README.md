Definition:
A quarter-turn valve controlling fluid flow via a cylindrical or conical plug with a passageway is called a plug valve. The plug either lets fluid to flow through the pipe or blocks it as it spins inside the valve body.

Fluid pours readily via the valve when the opening in the plug matches the pipeline. The aperture turns away from the flow path when the plug is turned 90 degrees, hence completely stopping the flow. The valve may be opened and closed rapidly thanks to this basic rotation mechanism.

Plug valves are frequently employed in systems needing quick isolation and low leakage since they have a simple design and effective sealing ability. 

Working Principle of a Plug Valve:
Based on the revolution of the plug within the valve body, a plug valve's working mechanism is driven. The plug spins with help from a stem and actuator.

The plug turns inside the body as the valve handle is rotated 90 degrees. When the opening in the plug matches the pipeline, the valve allows the fluid to pass without obstruction. Turning the plug so that the opening is perpendicular to the pipeline totally stops the flow.

This quarter-turn mechanism lets one rapidly close and open the valve. Due of its basic construction and few moving components, the plug valve provides dependable sealing and effective flow control in several industrial situations.
